Data structure

The syllabus for Data Structures typically includes the following topics:

1. Introduction to Data Structures: Definitions, types, and importance of data structures.

2. Arrays: Representation, operations, and applications.

3. Linked Lists: Singly, doubly, and circular linked lists, operations, and use cases.

4. Stacks: Definition, operations, and applications such as expression evaluation and backtracking.

5. Queues: Types of queues (simple, circular, priority, and dequeues), operations, and applications.

6. Trees: Binary trees, binary search trees, AVL trees, B-trees, operations, and applications.

7. Graphs: Representation (adjacency list and matrix), traversal techniques (BFS and DFS), shortest path algorithms (Dijkstra’s and Bellman-Ford), and applications.

8. Hashing: Hash functions, collision resolution techniques, and applications.

9. Heaps: Binary heaps, operations, and applications.

10. Sorting Algorithms: Bubble sort, selection sort, insertion sort, merge sort, quick sort, and heap sort.

11. Searching Algorithms: Linear search, binary search, and their complexities.

12. File Structures: Sequential, indexed, and direct access files.

13. Advanced Data Structures: Trie, segment tree, and suffix tree.
